We present \texttt{micrograd}, an open-source FEniCSx framework for
density-based topology optimisation of systems governed by coupled
Brinkman--convection-diffusion equations.
The principal contribution is a complete continuous adjoint formulation
for a multi-objective cost functional coupling viscous dissipation
and outlet concentration mismatch, with consistent SUPG stabilisation
applied to both the forward and adjoint transport equations at
high cell P\'{e}clet numbers ($\Pe\sim10^2$--$10^5$).

The framework is implemented in FEniCSx~(DOLFINx~v0.7.3) using
Taylor--Hood P2/P1 elements for flow and P1+SUPG elements for transport.
Robust convergence is achieved through five nested continuation strategies:
Helmholtz PDE filtering, Heaviside $\beta$-continuation
to $\beta=128$, logarithmic $\alpha_{\max}$ ramping,
and a hybrid OC/MMA optimisation schedule.
Functional smoothness is confirmed by a finite-difference Taylor remainder
test (slope $\approx 2.0$ on both coarse and primary meshes);
the continuously assembled adjoint captures the correct descent direction
(Pearson correlation $\geq 0.80$ with finite-difference gradients).

As a benchmark, the framework is applied to porous microfluidic mixing
optimisation on an $80\times20$ mesh.
The optimised permeability distribution achieves outlet concentration
RMSE of $\rmseTopOpt$ (linear target), $\rmseSigmoid$ (sigmoid), and
$\rmseGaussian$ (Gaussian peak), demonstrating framework generality
across target profiles without modifying the adjoint structure.
The gray-zone fraction is reduced to $\grayFrac$ via extended
$\beta$-continuation; threshold-projection to binary-permeability
and experimental validation are future directions.
The complete pipeline runs on a standard laptop in under thirty minutes
and is fully reproducible via Docker and continuous integration.
