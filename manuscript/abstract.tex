We present \texttt{micrograd}, an open-source FEniCSx implementation
of density-based topology optimisation for coupled Brinkman--convection-diffusion systems.
The implementation integrates a continuous adjoint for a multi-objective cost functional
coupling viscous dissipation and outlet concentration mismatch,
consistent SUPG stabilisation of both forward and adjoint transport equations
at high P\'{e}clet numbers ($\Pe\sim10^2$--$10^5$),
and a modular continuation schedule that reliably reduces the gray-zone fraction
from $\grayShortRun$ to $\grayFrac$ over $\nIterPrimary$ iterations.
Gradient direction is verified by finite-difference Taylor test (slope $\approx 2.0$)
and Pearson correlation $\geq 0.80$ with finite-difference gradients;
the OC and MMA optimisers depend only on gradient sign, confirmed numerically
across six orders of magnitude of gradient scaling (Table~\ref{tab:scaling}).

As a benchmark, the implementation achieves outlet RMSE~$=\rmseTopOpt$
for a linear concentration gradient on an $80\times20$ mesh,
with framework generality demonstrated on sigmoid (RMSE~$=\rmseSigmoid$)
and Gaussian peak (RMSE~$=\rmseGaussian$) targets.
\texttt{micrograd} optimises within the Brinkman--SIMP porous-medium model;
the optimised permeability field is \emph{not} a manufacturable design.
Hard-thresholding to binary permeability increases RMSE by $\binaryGapPct\%$,
confirming that the surrogate exploits gray-zone diffusion unavailable
in physical geometries.
Users requiring manufacturable designs should apply robust projection
methods~\cite{wang2011} during optimisation.
The complete pipeline is reproducible in approximately one hour on a standard laptop
from a single Docker command and is archived with a Zenodo DOI~\cite{micrograd2025zenodo}.
