\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We have presented \texttt{micrograd}, an open-source FEniCSx framework
for density-based topology optimisation of coupled
Brinkman--convection-diffusion systems.
The key methodological contributions are:
\begin{itemize}
    \item a complete continuous adjoint derivation for the coupled system,
          with exact chain-rule propagation through the Helmholtz filter
          and Heaviside projection, verified by a Taylor remainder test
          exhibiting second-order convergence ($\text{slope} = 2.01$);
    \item consistent SUPG stabilisation for both the forward and adjoint
          transport equations, with gradient-error verification;
    \item a smooth $C^\infty$ penalty replacing non-differentiable
          concentration clipping, ensuring adjoint consistency;
    \item five nested continuation strategies that together ensure reliable
          convergence to connected, near-binary designs; and
    \item full reproducibility via Docker, continuous integration, and
          Zenodo archival.
\end{itemize}

As a proof-of-concept, the framework automatically discovers a branching
channel topology achieving an outlet RMSE of $\rmseTopOpt$ for a linear
concentration target and reducing hydraulic resistance by $\reduction\%$
relative to the classic Christmas-tree baseline --- demonstrating that
the coupled adjoint successfully navigates the high-dimensional design
space.

The framework is designed to be general: any scalar objective that is
a differentiable functional of the velocity and concentration fields can
be incorporated by modifying the objective and adjoint boundary conditions
in the UFL description.
This generality, combined with a computational cost of under twenty
minutes on a laptop and full reproducibility, makes \texttt{micrograd}
a practical foundation for computational microfluidic inverse design.
